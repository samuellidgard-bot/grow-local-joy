# Carousel 04 - If You Do Not Track Leads, You Guess

## Goal

Explain why Xello includes tracking as part of marketing foundations.

## Slide 1

Headline:
If You Do Not Track Leads,
You Guess.

Subline:
Good marketing needs clear feedback.

Visual:
Dashboard/tracker graphic.

## Slide 2

Headline:
Likes are not leads.

Body:
A post can look busy and still create no useful enquiries.

Visual:
Likes icon separated from enquiry icon.

## Slide 3

Headline:
Track where every enquiry came from.

Body:
Website, Instagram, Google, referral, Facebook, TikTok or ads.

Visual:
Source map/funnel.

## Slide 4

Headline:
Track project fit.

Body:
Is it the right type of job, location, value and timeframe?

Visual:
Good-fit / poor-fit cards.

## Slide 5

Headline:
Track follow-up.

Body:
Good leads go cold when nobody replies quickly or clearly.

Visual:
Clock + message icon.

## Slide 6

Headline:
The goal is better conversations.

Body:
Not more noise, random messages or low-fit jobs.

Visual:
Conversation bubbles with green check.

## Slide 7

Headline:
Marketing gets better when the data is clear.

Body:
Follow @xello_media for practical systems that turn attention into enquiries.

## Caption

You cannot improve what you do not track.

For home improvement businesses, the useful question is not just "did we get attention?"

It is:

- where did the enquiry come from?
- what job type is it?
- is it a good fit?
- how fast did we reply?
- did it become a quote?

That is why lead tracking is part of the Xello foundations process.
