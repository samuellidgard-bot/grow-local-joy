# Xello Faceless Social Content Machine

Created: 2026-05-08

Purpose: give Xello Media a consistent, useful social presence before Sam is ready to post regular founder-led videos.

This is a faceless content system for business owners who want practical tips about marketing, AI, websites, social media and lead generation.

## Positioning

Xello should not post generic agency tips.

Xello should post short, useful lessons for local service businesses:

- How to look easier to trust online.
- How to use AI without making the business look fake.
- How to turn content into enquiries.
- How to fix weak websites, bios, CTAs and follow-up.
- How to understand social media without chasing vanity metrics.

Core line:

```text
Practical marketing, AI and social media lessons for local businesses that want better enquiries.
```

## Current Strategy Notes

- Short-form video and educational social content remain the main discovery format, but the content needs to be useful enough to hold attention rather than only trend-chasing.
- Faceless AI-assisted content can work for education, but avoid fake people, fake client stories and fake authority. Trust is the point.
- AI-generated or AI-assisted content should be labelled or disclosed when platform rules require it.
- Direct publishing automation should stay approval-first until platform account access, consent, API requirements and brand QA are properly set up.

## Faceless Formats

Use these repeatable formats:

1. Text-led Reel
   - Background: clean brand colour, simple motion, subtle screen recording or stock-free abstract shapes.
   - Structure: hook, 3 points, one action.

2. Screen-recording tip
   - Show a website, CRM, Google Business example, AI prompt, analytics screen or content plan.
   - Blur private client info.

3. Carousel
   - Slide 1: strong hook.
   - Slides 2-5: practical points.
   - Final slide: save/follow/action CTA.

4. AI tool breakdown
   - Show one useful business use case.
   - Keep it practical, not hype-led.

5. Mini audit without naming
   - "A local business could improve this..."
   - Do not shame businesses or reveal private data.

6. Myth vs truth
   - Short, punchy, easy to understand.

7. Checklist post
   - Give a simple process the viewer can copy.

## Content Pillars

### 1. Marketing Foundations

Help businesses understand what to fix before paid ads.

Examples:

- Your Instagram bio should answer these 3 things.
- Before spending on ads, check your enquiry route.
- Why your website needs one obvious next step.

### 2. AI For Small Businesses

Show useful, responsible AI workflows.

Examples:

- 3 safe ways a local business can use AI this week.
- AI can draft replies, but should not pretend to be you.
- How to use AI to turn messy notes into content ideas.

### 3. Social Media That Creates Trust

Teach social content as proof, not entertainment for its own sake.

Examples:

- Show process, not just finished results.
- Why "quality work" is weaker than visible proof.
- How one project can become five posts.

### 4. Better Enquiries

Focus on lead quality, not just more attention.

Examples:

- Cheap leads can waste your time.
- A useful enquiry includes photos, area, project type and timeline.
- Track where every enquiry came from.

### 5. Build In Public Without Being The Face Yet

Show Xello's thinking while staying faceless.

Examples:

- What I am building into the CRM today.
- One lesson from working with local service businesses.
- A simple content system I would use for a trade business.

## Publishing Rule

The automation is allowed to draft.

The automation is not allowed to publish, schedule, comment, DM, spend money or connect social accounts without Sam's approval.

Reason: social platforms do support publishing APIs in some cases, but direct posting requires account authorization, platform/API requirements and content consent. TikTok's Content Posting API, for example, requires explicit consent and unaudited clients are restricted to private visibility until audit. Keep Xello safe by making the first version approval-first.

## Brand Style

Use:

- Black, white, grey and Xello green.
- Clean bold text.
- Small X logo corner bug.
- Simple progress bars, checklist ticks, screen-recording frames and arrows.
- No fake AI avatars pretending to be people.
- No generic stock-business imagery.

Avoid:

- Purple-blue AI slop visuals.
- Fake "guru" money claims.
- Overpromising leads.
- Shaming local businesses.
- Posting client-private data.
